Our goals

We started by developing an understanding of what the space needed to do, digging into member feedback and understanding stakeholder issues and goals. Initial research and analysis informed our foundational goals for the experience.

  • Simplify with purpose
  • Personalize the experience
  • Be helpful and proactive

How did these goals take form?

We can see our goals come to life through the structure, visual design, and offerings of the page.

The structure is composed of two high level sections, products (left column) and activity (right column). This simple division sets clear expectations for where to find information, drastically improving navigation.

The visual design takes a minimalist approach, bringing the most important data to the forefront and allowing members to focus on the task at hand without the distractions of irrelevant promotions and overbearing branding.

Arguably the most important process, what the page offers was critically analyzed. Assessing and filtering which elements deserved to live on the page immediately created a simpler experience and establishing strict standards for future content add requests allows us to preserve simplicity and focus over time.

What further complexities must the structure accommodate?

USAA members have a wide variety of relationships with the company and unique ways of organizing their stuff. Currently, members have anywhere between 0 and nearly 100 bank accounts, insurance policies, and aggregated investments. On top of this, members might hide accounts, create groups, or see asset and liability totals depending on their product mix.

Our solution to accommodating any number of products was to offer an expanded card state, when needed. This way, if a member has 6 or more products in a card, they are able to see some of their stuff in the collapsed state and the rest in the expanded state.

This allows members to access all their stuff from one page without detracting from the value USAA provides through activity cards or the businessâ€™ marketing needs. This model also allowed us to avoid confusing interactions when integrating our customization features.
